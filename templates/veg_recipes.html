Why Choose Vegetarian?

The Rich Tradition of Indian Vegetarian Cuisine

India has one of the world's oldest and richest vegetarian culinary traditions. From the creamy paneer dishes of North India to the coconut-infused curries of the South, vegetarian Indian food offers incredible variety and depth of flavor.

With a focus on whole grains, legumes, fresh vegetables, and aromatic spices, Indian vegetarian cuisine is not only delicious but also nutritionally complete.

Health Benefits:

  • Rich in protein from legumes and dairy
  • High in dietary fiber
  • Packed with antioxidants from spices
  • Lower in saturated fat

Recipe Title

30 mins

Ingredients

  • 2 cups paneer, cubed
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/4 cup cream
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat butter in a pan, add onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  3. Add tomato puree, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until oil separates.
  4. Add paneer cubes and cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add cream and garam masala. Simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or rice.
